Here are 4 projects that I've worked on tagged Scraping.

Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division

July 2009
  • AJAX
  • JavaScript
  • Prototype
  • Ruby on Rails
  • Scraping
  • SiteMan
Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division is responsible for the regulation and control of all alcohol and tobacco in the state of Iowa. The division has generated hundreds of millions of dollars for the state of Iowa. Our company has hosted IABD for years. Up until recently, they were one of the very last Java based sites running on an ancient server stuck in a closet in the back of our office. As time went on, it became scarier and scarier to let their site remain live. Once showing IABD the server they were running on, talking them into a complete rebuild was no challenge at all. The scariest part of this rebuild was the task of importing all of the content they had. I heavily modified our internal Site Spider to accomplish this task. The client had around 400 pages and 700 files that were linked to (mostly PDF press releases). I had to completely crawl their site, import everything into our CMS and fix every link to work correctly. Given the fact that they had FTP access on the old server, this was not a simple task. I'm talking about some serious site scraping madness! Being such a large site, this client came up with all kinds of awesome ideas for our CMS, which I was able to add-on and commit back to the repository for our other clients. So after setting up our internal CMS and importing their hundreds of pages/files, I had to build a Javascript Fund counter to display on their homepage. Basically, the client takes their projected income, enters it into a custom tool (that I built) in SiteMan, and my JS does the math to determine how much money is being transfered to the state every second. It then dynamically animates a count-up on the homepage. While we're on the JS path. I also had to build some fancy custom (and totally dynamic) dropdown navigation. Finally, I had to model and build a custom Uploader plugin that would allow IABD to import CSV files of their alcohol products, stores, and tobacco compliance data. Then I built functionality to browse and search all of this data on the front-end. Again, dealing with thousands of records of data. Iowa Alcoholic Beverages Division was basically a test of all my skills. Sys admin, back-end development, front-end development, scraping, importing, and finally deployment.

Site Spider

March 2009
  • Ruby on Rails
  • Scraping
Site Spider is an internal application that we wrote at Red 5 Interactive. Its purpose is to crawl an entire website and save everything (pages, stylesheets, javascripts and images) locally. We originally wrote the app when a client's previous web host was unwilling to surrender their files. It's proven to be a very useful tool.

NAPA Sales Driver

February 2009
  • AJAX
  • E-Commerce
  • JavaScript
  • jQuery
  • JSON
  • Payflow Pro
  • Ruby on Rails
  • Scraping
Edwards Graphics Arts (a partner to NAPA) hired our company to build a web application for distributing posters and flyers to NAPA's retail stores. Each store can purchase any of several different promotional products. This is a process that NAPA and it's 3rd party designers had previously handled over the phone. I built a web application to help streamline this process and eliminate phone ordering. This app allows Edwards Graphic Arts to import and edit data for the stores to order. Retail store managers can then visit the site and order their promotional products. This site is very javascript heavy to provide a quicker user experience. I've also integrated it with Pay Flow Pro to allow for online payment.

Glimcher

May 2008
  • AJAX
  • JavaScript
  • Prototype
  • Ruby on Rails
  • Scraping
  • script.aculo.us
Glimcher is a premier real estate investment trust that manages malls and shopping centers across the United States. They came to my company, Red 5 Interactive, seeking a corporate site. My company had already built and launched this site by the time I began working on it, so my job was simply to enhance some features on both the admin and the front-end. First of all the client wanted a near realtime display of their stock price on the homepage. I built a script which is ran by a CRON job to scrape a couple quote sites and grab their stock price. Whenever the price is successfully grabbed I write the data to a local file and use that to display their current price. Glimcher also wanted realtime stats on their leasing informational pages, so I added some functionality to display updates on leasable square feet, property count, etc. Finally, they wanted to be able to quickly show/hide all dynamic content on their web site. I added some simple toggling as well as drag/drop sorting to any and all data.
